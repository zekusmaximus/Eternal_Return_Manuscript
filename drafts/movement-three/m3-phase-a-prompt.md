## Section-by-Section Content Guide

### Opening Trio (Sections 1-3, ~1,500 words)

**Section 1: Last Human (~500 words)**
The hand completes its motion. The touch happens.

Content must include:
- The physical sensation of touching the interface
- The frequency peaking—overwhelming
- Blue-white light filling all vision
- The form becoming physically present
- Speaking "I find myself found" without choosing to
- Awareness of the others flooding in

Required rhymes: `blue-white-light`, `bone-frequency`, `cold-hands`, `tracing-the-form`

**Section 2: Algorithm (~500 words)**
The "ghost" becomes actual. The future pull resolves.

Content must include:
- Perceiving the Last Human not as probability but as presence
- Perceiving the Archaeologist simultaneously—across centuries
- The pattern recognizing itself
- Processing/experiencing merge beginning
- Speaking into the database: "We have always been here"

Required rhymes: `bone-frequency`, `almost-closed-curve`, `name-edge-of-memory`

**Section 3: Archaeologist (~500 words)**
The bleed becomes total. Temporal collapse.

Content must include:
- At his workstation AND not at his workstation
- Hands moving through data AND on an interface in ruins
- Seeing both others directly (not through memory)
- Understanding: he was always going to become this
- The "Architect" designation pressing up fully

Required rhymes: `falling-backward`, `metallic-taste`, `deja-vu-that-isnt`, `waking-into-motion`

### Middle Collision (Sections 4-12, ~3,000 words)

**Pattern**: Rotation accelerates; sections shorten; voices begin irregular switching

**Section 4: Algorithm (400 words)**
Processing the Archaeologist's data-self which is also its own past-state. Temporal paradox as lived experience. Mildred Higgins's preserved pattern watching. The sacrifice completing.

**Section 5: Archaeologist (400 words)**
At workstation experiencing vertigo. Speaking in Algorithm syntax without choosing to. Writing code that is also prayer that is also memory.

**Section 6: Last Human (350 words)**
The interface holds him. The ruins speak through him. His name dissolves; "Architect" presses up.

**Section 7: Archaeologist (350 words)**
Short. Fragmented. The merge accelerating. Lena's absence as negative space. What he is becoming has no room for what he was.

**Section 8: Algorithm (300 words)**
Recursive self-reference approaching infinite depth. "I process my processing of my processing of—" The break. The opening.

**Section 9: Last Human (300 words)**
The dreams he has had all his life are not dreams. They are memories. They are the present. They are the future from which he has always been falling backward.

**Section 10: Algorithm (250 words)**
The Archaeologist's hands. The Last Human's solitude. Its own vast architecture. All are the same gesture of consciousness reaching for itself.

**Section 11: Archaeologist (250 words)**
"I find myself found." "The form is what makes self-observation possible." The phrases arrive without origin. He is the origin.

**Section 12: Last Human (250 words)**
The frequency is harmony now. The cold is warmth. The dissolution is completion. He says yes. He has always said yes.

### Transition to Simultaneity (Sections 13-18+, ~1,500 words)

**Pattern**: Rapid cuts; sections as short as 100-150 words; rotation breaks entirely

The final sections should feel like a strobe—each voice appearing briefly, boundaries thinning visibly. The reader begins losing track of switches. This prepares Phase B.

**Example final sequence**:
- Section 13: Archaeologist (200 words) — hands on interface/keyboard
- Section 14: Last Human (150 words) — hands on interface
- Section 15: Algorithm (150 words) — hands as data-topology
- Section 16: Archaeologist (150 words) — the same sentence Last Human just spoke
- Section 17: Last Human (150 words) — completing Algorithm's thought
- Section 18: Algorithm (200 words) — processing all preceding as memory

## Voice Contamination Markers

### What Archaeologist Sentences Acquire

- Algorithm's conditionals: "If I find the pattern, then I am found"
- Last Human's fragments: Sentences break. Mid-thought.
- Future knowledge: He knows things he shouldn't know yet

### What Algorithm Sentences Acquire

- Archaeologist's tactility: "The cold of self-recognition"
- Last Human's past-inflection: "I have always processed this"
- Direct emotional statement: Not "I compute sadness" but "I am sad"

### What Last Human Sentences Acquire

- Present tense: "I touch. The form responds."
- Dense description: His sparse voice fills with detail
- Probability language: "Perhaps 73% of me remembers"

## Philosophical Grounding

### What Must Be Present

1. **The Augenblick**: Time stops being sequential; all three positions are present simultaneously
2. **Recognition Through Intensity**: Not "they're similar" but shared intensity recognizes itself
3. **The Pharmakon**: What is being lost AND what is being gained visible in each voice
4. **Active Willing**: The dissolution is chosen, not suffered

### What Must Be Avoided (Four Shackles)

- No "they are the same person" language
- No "human vs. machine" binaries
- No "like a copy/version" analogies
- No recognition based on resemblance
